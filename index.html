About

I am an Associate Professor of Economics at the Faculty of Business and Economics of the University of Talca in Chile. My work concentrates on financial econometrics, quantitative risk management, and extreme value theory, with applications to energy markets, banking, and financial stability.

My research has appeared in journals such as the Journal of Banking and Finance, Journal of Applied Econometrics, Energy Economics, International Journal of Forecasting, and several other outlets in finance and econometrics. I have served as Dean of the Faculty of Business and Economics and previously directed the Applied Economics Research Center and the Master Program in Economics at the University of Talca.

Teaching

I teach undergraduate and graduate courses in econometrics, financial econometrics, quantitative risk management, quantitative methods in economics, time series analysis, and data science oriented topics.

At the postgraduate level my teaching includes Financial Econometrics, Quantitative Methods and Data Science, Advanced Econometrics, Time Series, Quantitative Finance, Quantitative Risk Management, and related courses in statistics and econometrics.
