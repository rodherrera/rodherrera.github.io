Rodrigo Herrera

Publications Working papers Research grants CV

I am an Associate Professor of Economics at the University of Talca, Chile, affiliated with the School of Business and Economics. My research focuses on financial econometrics, extreme value theory, and quantitative risk management, with particular emphasis on modeling the dynamics of extreme events and systemic tail risk in financial markets.

My work has been published in journals such as the Journal of Banking and Finance, Journal of Applied Econometrics, International Journal of Forecasting, Journal of Empirical Finance, and Energy Economics. I served as Dean of the School from 2018 to 2024 and have been principal investigator of several FONDECYT research projects on high-frequency data, tail risk, and multivariate extreme value models.

I hold a PhD in Economics from TU Dresden in Germany and have held visiting research appointments at the University of Oxford, the University of Edinburgh, the Warsaw School of Economics (SGH), and Queensland University of Technology (QUT).
